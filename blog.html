Essential Information for Your Bali Trip

Planning a trip to Bali, Indonesia? Get ready for an unforgettable adventure filled with rich cultural experiences, tantalizing local cuisine, and breathtaking natural wonders. Here's all the essential information you need to make the most of your Bali getaway.

Temple Etiquette

When visiting Balinese temples, it's essential to observe proper etiquette to show respect for the sacredness of the site and the local culture.

Firstly, dress modestly by wearing clothing that covers your shoulders and knees. Avoid wearing revealing or beachwear attire out of respect for the religious significance of the temple. Additionally, before entering a temple, you may be required to wear a sarong (a traditional wrap-around cloth) and a sash, which are often provided at the temple entrance.

Upon entering the temple grounds, remember to remove your shoes before stepping onto the sacred grounds. Look for signs or follow the lead of locals to determine if this practice applies.

Once inside, maintain a quiet and respectful demeanor, refraining from loud conversations, using your phone, or engaging in disruptive behavior. It's important to follow any rituals or ceremonies taking place in the temple, observing quietly and respectfully from a distance. If unsure about proper conduct, follow the lead of locals or ask for guidance from temple staff.

Local Cuisine

Balinese cuisine is renowned for its bold flavors, aromatic spices, and fresh ingredients. To fully immerse yourself in the culinary delights of Bali, be sure to sample some of the island's signature dishes at local eateries known as warungs.

Start your day with a hearty breakfast of nasi goreng (fried rice) or mie goreng (fried noodles) served with a side of sambal (spicy chili paste) and a fried egg on top.


For lunch or dinner, don't miss the opportunity to try lawar (a traditional vegetable salad), and sambal matah (a spicy chili relish).

Explore Local Attractions

  • Kecak Dance, Uluwatu
    Witness the mesmerizing Kecak Dance at Uluwatu, where performers enact scenes from the Ramayana against a stunning sunset backdrop, accompanied by rhythmic chanting.
  • Dance Performance At Ubud Palace, Ubud
    Enjoy a traditional dance performance at Ubud Palace, featuring graceful movements and vibrant costumes that bring Balinese legends to life in a majestic setting.
  • Barong and Keris Dance, Batubulan
    Experience the dramatic Barong and Keris Dance in Batubulan, a captivating portrayal of the eternal battle between good and evil, complete with elaborate costumes and enchanting music.
  • Shadow Puppet (Wayang Kulit), Bangli
    Discover the ancient art of Wayang Kulit at Oka Kartini workshop in Bangli, where master puppeteers showcase intricate leather puppets and perform classic tales from Hindu epics with traditional gamelan accompaniment.
